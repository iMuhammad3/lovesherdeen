back

On Eid day I randomly had this urge to travel to Kaduna and so I travelled the next day. I don’t understand how it was that my parents easily agreed considering that I told them I wanted to leave the next day. But my parents are like that and I’m glad.
I called my uncle to let him know that I was coming to his house there in Kaduna, he said he’d love for me to come but it wasn’t really a good time. He continued saying he had just divorced his wife. I told him I was aware of all that and he said well then I was welcome. After that conversation I considered cancelling because I had thoughts that I wasn’t really welcome, and though I could’ve also just crashed at my other cousins house, the main reason I wanted to go there was because of him. So I pushed those negative thoughts away, packed my clothes for two days and booked my ticket for tomorrow’s train.
Well, I tried to book the tickets but I couldn’t. To book a train ticket I’m required to use my NIN and the phone number that was linked to my NIN. But I lost the phone number linked to my NIN a long time ago. I tried changing the phone number on the NIN website, I also couldn’t do that because my NIN was still classified as minor’s NIN. I’m not sure why it doesn’t automatically upgrade when I turned 18. So, to upgrade I’d have to go to the NIN office. Even now that I have the time to do that I’m too lazy, and I’m shy as well. Anyway, all that wasn’t going to stop me from fulfilling my random desire to travel to Kaduna. I informed my dad and he said I should go to the train station early and buy a ticket there. I felt weird being driven by the bolt driver as we were on the way to the train station, normally I’m the one always driving but for the first time in a while I became the passenger princess. I arrived at the station and asked where I could buy a ticket. I was directed and when I gave my information to buy the ticket I had the same problem as I did when I first tried to book online. They advised me to buy a business minor ticket. First of all it was business class which was about 3000 naira more than the normal ticket, and second it’s a minor’s ticket, meaning I had to be accompanied by an adult. It made no sense why buying a minor ticket was a solution to my issue or why it had to be business class instead of the normal minor ticket. But I just wanted to have my ticket and leave the place so I didn’t ask any questions. I bought the ticket and waited for the time to travel.
We boarded the train as soon as it arrived. There were very few people traveling, after all who travels from Abuja to Kaduna a day after Eid? At some point I thought about how I was going to get a ticket back to Abuja. It was a genuine concern, considering that I only planned to spend two days, and that was a week where everyone who travelled to Kaduna earlier would be rushing to come back. I checked the train website and to my surprise, most of the tickets for the next few days had been sold out. I then refrained from thinking about that for the rest of the journey. I focused on my train ride. I sat in silence the whole trip, thinking about who knows what. 3 plus hours of sitting and doing nothing. There was empty seats next to me, and one woman across the table, so I was quite comfortable. It was my first time travelling by myself. Though, it didn’t feel strange. It felt… normal. Maybe cause I’ve always preferred to do things by myself.
We arrived safely at Kaduna, my uncle hadn’t arrived at the station yet so I went to pray. Oh how happy I am when I get to pray 2 raka’ats instead of 4. After praying I looked for my shoes where I left them but they weren’t there. My first thought was that my first experience in Kaduna was having my shoes stolen. I looked around and saw a shoe shiner shining the shoes. I was relieved. I don’t remember why but I didn’t get a chance to thank him or give him money. I left to find my uncle and we hugged, entered his car and took off to his house.
The next day was a really fun day. It started with my uncle worrying that I was going to be too bored at his house. I told him I like to embrace boredom, or maybe that’s what I say because I want people to think I’m wise or something. Anyway, after praying Dhuhr, I walked around the area. I had seen on google maps there was a chicken republic near by. I walked to the place but there was no chicken republic, just an abandoned building. I felt betrayed by the same trust worthy google maps who had always led me to places I’d never been in Abuja. I walked a few more meters there and behold, there was a chicken republic. I bought and enjoyed their sandwich. It was a hundred naira cheaper than the ones in Abuja but the quality was slightly not as good as Abuja. After that I found a park on google maps that was also near by. I hopped on a bike to get their. The place looked nothing like the pictures I saw on the map. I quickly left before the craziness of the kids swimming in the nasty pool would rub off on me. I went back home after my mini exploration. My uncle said he was going to take us bowling at some new place. We went together with Yasir. I think Yasir is my mom’s cousin, though he looks about my age. At the bowling place I had to briefly learn the rules and techniques from chatgpt. I had never bowled in my life so I didn’t want to look stupid in the presence of the fine girls at the fancy bowling place. I learned and adapted quickly and got 2nd place just few points behind my uncle. After having some drinks and leaving the place, my uncle took me to my other cousins house from my father’s side where I was meant to spend my last night. That same the day, in the evening I went out with my cousins to a fancy restaurant owned by my father’s friends wife. They gave us free tea and we chilled there for a while before heading to another food place to buy burgers and shawarma. On our way back home, we stopped by at a house and they gave us meat and stuff. Then, as we left the house, the car stopped on the road. We had to push the car off the road and figure out the problem. It was the battery. We walked back to the other house to borrow their battery and start our car. We started the car and went back home. Many other things happened that day, good and bad, it was a weird day.
It was the day for me to leave, but I didn’t have a ticket and all tickets for the day were sold out. I discussed with my dad and he asked his friend about it who then asked his friend at the station if I could get a ticket. He said yes, I should meet with him early to get someone else’s ticket. So, when it was time I booked a bolt ride to the train station and found Oga Tanko, who gave me a ticket with a name like “Fatima Dahiru”. “Am I really gonna get away with this?”, I thought, and I did. The officials and the boarding place and the ones inside the train only scan if the tickets are valid. They don’t care if you use someone else’s ticket. It was another 3 hours of sitting in silence but this time less comfortable and more noisy.
